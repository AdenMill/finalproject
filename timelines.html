Timelines

Here are brief descriptions of several notable Marvel comics timelines/alternate realities

  • Earth-616 (Main Marvel Universe): The primary continuity where most long-running titles and crossover events take place — the “default” Marvel history with the familiar line-up of heroes and villains.

  • Ultimate Universe (Earth-1610): A modern, streamlined reimagining of classic characters (launched in the early 2000s) that updates origins, tone, and continuity for new readers.

  • Age of Apocalypse: An alternate timeline where Professor X is killed, Apocalypse rises to power, and the X-Men fight to survive in a radically changed world.

  • Days of Future Past: A dystopian future in which mutants are hunted and imprisoned; many stories use time travel to try to prevent that outcome.

  • Marvel Zombies: A horror-flavored branch where many heroes become flesh-eating zombies, producing bleak, apocalyptic versions of familiar characters.

  • Marvel 2099: A futuristic line (e.g., Spider-Man 2099) set in the late 21st century with new characters and cyberpunk-flavored takes on Marvel concepts.

  • House of M: A reality-altering event (created by Scarlet Witch) that temporarily rewrites reality, producing a “what if” world where mutants dominate and personal histories change.

  • MC2 (Marvel Comics 2): A possible near-future timeline focusing on the next generation of heroes (notably Spider-Girl), exploring legacy and family-based storytelling.

  • Old Man Logan: A grim, far-future timeline where supervillains have won and an aged Wolverine travels a broken America; it spawned several sequels and inspired other media.

  • Marvel Noir / Spider-Verse / Other Pocket Universes: Marvel frequently spins off stylized one-shots or mini-universes (noir, manga, dystopian, gender-swapped versions like Spider-Gwen) that retell characters in different genres or eras.

    • List of major timelines and events

    Marvel comic timelines/events with the central characters for each and the key issues/mini‑series to read to get the core story.

  • Earth‑616 (Main continuity) Central characters: Spider‑Man (Peter Parker), Avengers core (Iron Man, Captain America, Thor), X‑Men (Cyclops, Wolverine), Fantastic Four. Key reads/events: Amazing Fantasy #15 (Spider‑Man origin), Fantastic Four #1 (1961), The Avengers #1 (1963), X‑Men #1 (1963), Secret Wars (1984) #1–12, Civil War (2006) #1–7, Infinity Gauntlet (1991) #1–6, House of M (2005) #1–8, Secret Invasion (2008) #1–8, recent X overhaul: House of X / Powers of X (2019).

  • Ultimate Universe (Earth‑1610) Central characters: Ultimate Spider‑Man (Peter Parker; later Miles Morales), Ultimates (Nick Fury, Captain America, Iron Man), Ultimate X‑Men. Key reads: Ultimate Spider‑Man #1 (2000 by Bendis), Ultimates #1 (2002), Ultimate X‑Men #1 (2001), Ultimate Fallout/Ultimate Comics Spider‑Man (intros to Miles Morales).

  • Age of Apocalypse (alternate X timeline) Central characters: Apocalypse, Magneto (leader), many X‑Men variants. Key reads: X‑Men: Alpha (1995) one‑shot, Age of Apocalypse core miniseries/1995 tie‑ins (look for “Age of Apocalypse” label and tie‑in runs like X‑Calibre, Gambit & Bishop).

  • Days of Future Past (dystopian X timeline) Central characters: Kitty Pryde, Wolverine, Sentinels, Rachel Summers. Key reads: The classic: Uncanny X‑Men #141–142 (1981).

  • House of M (reality‑warp event) Central characters: Scarlet Witch (Wanda Maximoff), Magneto, Avengers/X‑Men ensemble. Key reads: House of M #1–8 (2005) and the Decimation aftermath issues (e.g., New Avengers, X‑Men tie‑ins).

  • Secret Wars (1984) / Secret Wars (2015) Central characters: Wide cast across Marvel; 2015 focuses on multiversal mashups. Key reads: Secret Wars (1984) #1–12; Secret Wars (2015) #1–9 and major Battleworld tie‑ins (Old Man Logan, etc.).

  • Spider‑Verse / Spider‑Family events Central characters: Various Spider‑people (Peter Parker, Miles Morales, Spider‑Gwen, Spider‑Man 2099, etc.). Key reads: Edge of Spider‑Verse (various issues), Spider‑Verse (2014–2015) event, Spider‑Gwen first appearance: Edge of Spider‑Verse #2.

  • Old Man Logan / Dystopian Wolverine timelines Central characters: Logan (Old Man Logan), Hawkeye, villains as overlords. Key reads: Old Man Logan (2008) #1–6 (Mark Millar), Old Man Logan (2015) (Jeff Lemire) for later expansion.

  • Marvel 2099 Central characters: Miguel O’Hara (Spider‑Man 2099), Doom 2099, futuristic versions. Key reads: Spider‑Man 2099 #1 (1992), foundational 2099 titles (X‑Men 2099, Doom 2099).

  • Marvel Zombies Central characters: Zombified versions of Avengers and other heroes. Key reads: Marvel Zombies #1–5 (2005) and Marvel Zombies Return / sequels.

  • Cosmic/Annihilation era Central characters: Nova (Richard Rider), Silver Surfer, Star‑Lord, Thanos (in some arcs). Key reads: Annihilation (2006) #1–6, Annihilation: Conquest (2007) #1–6, Nova (2007) relaunch ties.

  • Planet Hulk / World War Hulk Central characters: Hulk (Bruce Banner), the Red King, the Warbound. Key reads: Planet Hulk (2006) #1–5, World War Hulk (2007) #1–5.

  • Marvel 1602 (alternate history) Central characters: Shakespearean/Elizabethan versions of Spider‑Man, the X‑Men analogues, other heroes in 1602. Key reads: Marvel 1602 (2003) 8‑issue series (Neil Gaiman).
